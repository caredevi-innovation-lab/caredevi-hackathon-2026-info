CONDITION-TO-HOSPITAL-CAPABILITY MAPPING

This protocol maps suspected emergency conditions to required hospital capabilities for optimal patient routing.

CARDIAC ARREST (VF/VT/PEA/Asystole):
Required: cath_lab, icu, cardiology
Rationale: Post-ROSC STEMI requires immediate PCI. ICU for post-arrest care. Cardiology for rhythm management.
Time critical: 90-minute door-to-balloon for STEMI after ROSC.
Secondary: defibrillation available at all ERs — nearest hospital for initial stabilization if >15 min to cath lab.

STEMI (ST-Elevation Myocardial Infarction):
Required: cath_lab, cardiology
Strongly preferred: icu
Time critical: 90-minute door-to-balloon goal.
Rationale: Primary PCI is gold standard. Fibrinolytics only if no cath lab available within 120 min of first contact.

NSTEMI / Unstable Angina (High Risk):
Required: cardiology, icu
Preferred: cath_lab
Time: invasive strategy within 24h for high-risk, 72h for intermediate.

ISCHEMIC STROKE:
Required: comprehensive_stroke_center, neurology
For thrombectomy: add neurointerventional capability
Time critical: tPA window 4.5 hours, thrombectomy 6-24 hours.
Rationale: Comprehensive stroke centers have 24/7 neurology, CT/MRI, tPA protocols, and neurointerventional teams.

HEMORRHAGIC STROKE (ICH / SAH):
Required: neurosurgery, comprehensive_stroke_center
Preferred: icu, neurointerventional
Rationale: Surgical evacuation, VP shunt, or coil embolization may be needed. Aggressive BP management.

ANAPHYLAXIS (Severe):
Required: er_basic, icu
Rationale: Epinephrine and airway management at any ER. ICU for monitoring biphasic reaction and hemodynamic instability.

MAJOR TRAUMA (Multi-system / Penetrating / TBI):
Required: level_1_trauma, surgery, blood_bank
Preferred: icu, burn_unit (if burns involved)
Time critical: golden hour — definitive surgery within 60 minutes.
Rationale: Level 1 trauma centers have 24/7 trauma surgery, interventional radiology, and full blood bank.

HEAT STROKE:
Required: er_basic, icu
Rationale: Immediate cooling at any ER. ICU for complication management (rhabdomyolysis, DIC, renal failure).

PEDIATRIC EMERGENCY:
Required: pediatric_er, picu
Rationale: Pediatric-specific equipment, dosing, and training. Adult ERs may not have appropriate airway sizes, defibrillator settings, or medication doses.

BURNS (>20% TBSA or full-thickness):
Required: burn_unit, icu
Preferred: surgery, blood_bank

OBSTETRIC EMERGENCY:
Required: labor_and_delivery, obstetrics
Preferred: surgery (cesarean capability), icu (maternal ICU), blood_bank (massive transfusion for hemorrhage)

PSYCHIATRIC EMERGENCY:
Required: psychiatric_emergency
Preferred: er_basic (for medical clearance)

UNKNOWN / UNDIFFERENTIATED SEVERE:
Required: level_1_trauma, icu, er_basic
Rationale: Broadest capability set. When diagnosis is uncertain, route to highest capability center.
Exception: if patient is hemodynamically unstable — nearest hospital for stabilization, then transfer.

ROUTING PRIORITY ALGORITHM:
1. Filter hospitals by required capabilities (hard requirement).
2. If no full match: find best partial match (most required capabilities met).
3. For time-critical conditions: weight travel time heavily over ER wait time.
4. For stable conditions: balance travel time and ER wait time equally.
5. Always include 2 alternative hospitals in routing decision.
6. Re-evaluate routing if: patient deteriorates, hospital diverts, or ETA exceeds time-critical window.

DISTANCE THRESHOLDS:
For ESI 1 (cardiac arrest, hemorrhagic stroke): nearest hospital with defibrillator first, transfer after stabilization.
For ESI 1-2 time-critical (STEMI, ischemic stroke): bypass nearer hospital without required capabilities if time-critical window allows.
General rule: capability match > 30 min travel time penalty vs. closer hospital without capability.
